Indexing and Databases

All accepted papers presented at the ICNDEMAC 2026 Conference will be indexed in leading databases, including Scopus, IEEE Xplore, and Google Scholar. This ensures that your research gains global visibility and contributes to the academic and professional advancement in the field of Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE).

Guidelines for Authors

Authors submitting papers to the ICNDEMAC 2026 Conference in Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) must adhere to the following guidelines: Original Contributions: Papers must be original and not previously published. Experimental & Simulation Studies: Submissions should include experimental investigations, simulation studies with practical results, or case studies of industrial significance. Formatting: Authors should follow the provided paper formatting templates available on the conference website. Plagiarism Check:All submissions will undergo a plagiarism check to ensure originality. Paper Length: Papers should be within the specified word limit (usually 4 to 6 pages).
